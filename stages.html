Stages of the Big Bang

The Big Bang has been divided into several time periods or “epochs”. These epochs range from millions of years to the tiniest measurable amount of time.

  • Planck Epoch (from zero to approximately 10-43 seconds, 1 Planck time):

    • The four fundamental forces (Gravity, electromagnetism and the Strong and Weak Nuclear forces) all have the same strength.
    • The Universe is only 10-35 meters across and has an average temperature of around 1032°C.

  • Grand Unification Epoch (from 10–43 seconds to 10–36 seconds):

    • Gravity separates from the other fundamental forces
    • Basic fundamental particles (quarks and gluons) begin to form

  • Inflationary Epoch (10–36 seconds to 10 seconds):

    • The Strong nuclear force separates from the electroweak force, resulting in faster expansion
    • The elementary particles from the Grand Unification Epoch become spread thinly across the Universe, which is now around 10cm across.

  • Electroweak Epoch (10–36 seconds to 10–12 seconds):

    • New particles (W, Z and Higgs bosons) are created

    • Quark Epoch (10–12 seconds to 10–6 seconds):

      • Quarks, electrons and neutrinos begin to form in large numbers
    • The Universe cools to about 10 quadrillion degrees

  • Hadron Epoch (10-6 seconds to 1 second):

    • The Universe cools to around 1 trillion degrees, allowing quarks to form hadrons (protons and neutrons, the essential components of matter)

  • Lepton Epoch (1 second to 3 minutes):

    • Most hadrons and anti-hadrons (antimatter version of hadrons) have annihilated each other
    • Electrons and positrons (antimatter version of electrons) begin to annihilate each other

  • Nucleosynthesis Epoch (3 minutes to 20 minutes):

    • The temperature of the Universe reaches 1 billion degrees
    • Hydrogen, Helium and Lithium nuclei begin to form
    • After around 20 minutes, the temperature is too low for nuclear fusion

  • Photon Epoch (Radiation Domination, 3 minutes to 240000 years)

    • The universe is full of plasma (a soup of nuclei and electrons)
    • The Universe is dominated by photons (particles of light)

  • Recombination Epoch (240000 to 300000 years):

    • Temperature falls to around 3000 degrees
    • Helium and hydrogen nuclei begin to join with electrons
    • The universe is now transparent, this is where the CMBR comes from

  • Dark Era (300000 to 150 million years)

    • First Generation stars
    • The universe is around 75% hydrogen and 24.5% Helium, with traces of Lithium

  • Reionization Epoch (150 million to 1 billion years):

    • First quasars form, re-ionizing the surrounding particles
    • Most of the matter in the universe is now plasma again

  • Formation Epoch (300 million years to 500 million years):

    • Stars and galaxies begin to form, and the spider-web of the cosmos begins to take shape
